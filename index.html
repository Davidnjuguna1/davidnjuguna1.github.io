About

I'm a Mechanical Engineering graduate from JKUAT with a broad foundation in structural design, propulsion systems, fluid mechanics, and electromechanical engineering. My journey spans the full design cycle — from CAD modelling to physical fabrication and systems testing.

My internship at the JKUAT Nakuja Rocket Project gave me rare exposure to aerospace engineering in Africa, where I worked on fin design, nose cone fabrication, parachute recovery systems, and launch infrastructure for the N-3 rocket.

As project lead on an Electric Vehicle design, I managed all mechanical subsystems including body structure, suspension geometry, transmission, braking, and thermal management — delivering complete CAD documentation.

I bring strong proficiency in Autodesk Inventor, SolidWorks, AutoCAD, ANSYS CFD/FEA, and embedded programming with Arduino and C++, combining analytical rigour with practical build experience.

Key Projects

P — 01
Electric Vehicle Design
Entered the SES (Society of Engineering Students) JKUAT Electric Vehicle Design Competition with a brief to design a fully electric saloon car. As the sole mechanical designer, I was responsible for every structural and mechanical subsystem: the vehicle chassis, body panels, transmission, suspension geometry, and cooling system — all modelled in Autodesk Inventor. The competition gave designers freedom to select motor, weight, and range specifications, so design decisions were driven by efficiency, structural integrity, and manufacturability. The project was a complete CAD and simulation exercise, with full engineering drawings produced for each subsystem.
Autodesk Inventor Chassis Design Suspension Transmission Cooling System SES Competition JKUAT
P — 02
Heated Vacuum Chamber with Agitator
Final year project — designed and built a heated vacuum chamber for drying avocado pulp prior to oil extraction. Operating conditions: 12.35 kPa vacuum at 50°C. The core engineering challenge was achieving a fully airtight chamber enclosure while still routing data cables out of the vessel for automated monitoring and control — requiring custom feedthrough seals. A second major challenge was integrating the agitation system inside the sealed chamber to keep the pulp in motion during the drying cycle. An Arduino-based relay control circuit was developed for automation. The chamber was successfully fabricated and tested.
Final Year Project SolidWorks Arduino Vacuum Systems 12.35 kPa / 50°C Agitation Design Automation Fabrication & Testing

CAD Assembly Animations

Both mechanisms were built from scratch following tutorials as part of a deliberate effort to expand CAD toolset beyond Autodesk Inventor. The animations demonstrate an understanding of kinematic relationships, mate configurations, and mechanical motion simulation.

P — 04
Airfoil CFD & FEA Analysis
Hobby simulation project completed as part of formal ANSYS training. Performed a coupled aerodynamic and structural analysis on an airfoil geometry: CFD for pressure distribution and wake flow visualisation, followed by FEA for total deformation, von-Mises equivalent stress, and Y-axis directional deformation under aerodynamic loading. The project served as a practical introduction to the full ANSYS simulation workflow — from geometry setup through to results interpretation.
ANSYS CFD ANSYS FEA Airfoil Analysis Von-Mises Stress Hobby
P — 05
Fin-Tube Heat Exchanger CFD
Hobby ANSYS Fluent project to develop proficiency in thermal CFD. Designed and simulated a fin-tube heat exchanger with the objective of heating an airstream to dry maize kernels — a practical agricultural application. Water was used as the working fluid on the tube side. Thermal contour results show the temperature gradient across the fin and tube surfaces (330–363 K), with residual convergence plots confirming solution stability across 200 solver iterations. The project strengthened skills in mesh setup, boundary condition definition, and post-processing of thermal results.
ANSYS Fluent Thermal CFD Fin-Tube HEX Maize Drying Water-Air Heat Transfer Hobby
P — 06
Shell & Tube Heat Exchanger CFD
Hobby project to extend ANSYS simulation skills into shell-and-tube heat exchanger analysis, following an established tutorial geometry. The full workflow was completed independently: CAD modelling in SolidWorks, mesh generation in ANSYS (913,808 nodes / 4,183,800 elements, Fluent solver, linear elements at 5 mm sizing), and thermal CFD with named selections for gas and water inlets/outlets. Temperature results show the hot-side gas cooling from 723 K inlet to outlet, with the shell-side coolant absorbing the heat load. More simulation images to follow.
SolidWorks ANSYS Fluent Shell & Tube HEX 4.18M Elements Thermal CFD Hobby
